PROJECT MAMMOTH

2021/22 / Installation

Project Mammoth is a hologram installation that welcomes viewers to learn about endangered marine species through the use of interactive technologies. As project lead and initial concept creator, I had the wonderful opportunity to manage a group of twelve amazing students, ensure the constant flow of progress amidst the installation's components, and contribute my own modelled assets for the installation and the website. Project Mammoth was featured in Brock University's Digital Scholarship Lab from April 8th to April 26th, 2022.

To learn more about Project Mammoth, its mission and the animals selected, visit the Project Mammoth website. To view information on the process of creation, as well as additional research and branding components, check out the Project Mammoth design document.

01/ CONCEPT

The idea to create a hologram was an interest of mine prior to the proposal portion of the project. Pitched to my peers as an installation that would utilize interactive media components and draw upon the strengths of group members, it received positive reception. Through planning sessions and detailed research, we selected endangered marine animals as our theme. Aiming to promote conservation efforts, we wanted to use the hologram to educate viewers and show how much our planet is affected by the actions of humanity.

02/ ASSETS

Given the complexity of the project itself, group members were assigned to smaller teams that would be responsible for generating the individual assets and components from the hologram. As a member of the modelling tream, I participated in the creation of 3D-modelled assets in Blender. Out of the eight low-poly animals we chose, I modelled the polar bear, African penguin, striped smooth hound shark and acropora jacquelinae coral.

Each animal model would then be placed into a corresponding environment model. Throughout the course of the project, the style of environments changed multiple times and the decision was made to select the polycarbonate globe as the final variant. I designed the environments for the African penguin, striped smooth hound shark, Hawksbill sea turtle, acropora jacquelinae coral and North Atlantic right whale. Once each globe was formatted, individual images were rendered using Blender's Cycles renderer.

* Blue-footed booby model/environment and marine iguana model environment by Samantha Savage. Polar bear environment and Hawksbill sea turtle model/environment by Will Paskulin. North Atlantic right whale model by Xu Yankeer.

03/ ANIMATIONS

Animating camera movements within Blender and integrating a moving component to the visual assets was a key step in making the hologram believable. Each globe environment was animated to reveal the animal inside, then rendered as a .MKV file and converted to .MP4. To respect the physics associated with how hologram prisms reflect light and images, video renders were formatted into what our group dubbed "hologram videos". These consisted of an image being duplicated four times and rotated in different directions to display properly on the screen.
