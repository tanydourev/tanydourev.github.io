eco domes.
3D Design & Installation
The Eco Domes installation is an exploration into immersive environments and modelling for public spaces. Designed to occupy a portion of Queen Victoria Park in Niagara Falls, the structures serve the purpose of being interactible and accessible, allowing visitors to enter the shade provided and rest under a covered top. The installation has not been implemented within the park itself, but extensive research was done to simulate a production plan.
CREATED USING :
Rhino 7, Chaos V-Ray for Rhino, Blender
↓
PROCESS :
Designing the structures was split into a process of four steps: research existing dome-like structures and installations to understand their properties; create a simple structure using the panelling tools in Rhino 7; explore variations of materials offered by the V-Ray plugin; prepare a site plan, visualization and measurements for a simulated public integration.

A single structure consists of a torus-shaped dome with a concaved center, a hexagonally-panelled grid that overlays on top, a layered bottom section that allows for air flow and a central seating area that can house a biological element if desired (such as a large tree or floral arrangement).
In the Rhino 7 variation, cream-coloured plastic, glass and light-toned wood were used as materials. Due to limitations with V-Ray, the glass material replaced the intended polycarbonate material, however in the Blender variation, polycarbonate has been appropriately assigned to the dome.
↓
SITE PLAN :
The proposed location for this installation is Queen Victoria Park in Niagara Falls, a public park that is home to the city's annual Festival of Lights. Throughout the summer months, the park serves multiple functions and is fluid in its usage.
If the installation were to be implemented, four structures would be positioned across the back portion of the park. Given the area is exposed to sunlight and contains little tree cover, the polycarbonate dome would work in combination with the layered panelling to allow for a constant flow of air during the warm weather and insulation during the cooler months of the year.
