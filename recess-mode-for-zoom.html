Recess Mode for Zoom™
Improving the online learning experience for elementary students by bringing recess and playtime to virtual meeting programs.
↓
PROJECT TYPE

UX/UI case study

TIMELINE

2021 - 2022, 2024

CONTRIBUTIONS

UX researcher, data analysis & documentation, UI designer

CREDITS

Tany Dourev, Sydney Cowling, Julia Correia, Jaiden Armstrong, Jakob Visic

Overview

Recess Mode is a conceptual feature for video call software Zoom™ that aims to improve the educational experience of elementary school students during the COVID-19 pandemic. Using a virtual space that simulates recess, students are encouraged to develop social skills with their peers and in turn, help ease the burden on parents/guardians and teachers.

The Brief:

How can we improve the online learning experience for elementary school students?

Research Plan & Methods

We began by conducting interview with parents/guardians and teachers to gain a better understanding of the issues students were experiencing as a result of the transition to online learning. Our criteria for candidates included the following: the students of parents/guardians had to be learning remotely from home; teachers were required to be teaching primarily using remote/digital methods.
A total of ten (10) participants were interviewed by each member of the team. Interviews were conducted via digital methods in compliance with the public health guidelines and regulations set forth by Health Canada. In accordance with guidelines for ethical research set forth by the TCPS 2: CORE document and the Government of Canada, students and children under the legal age of 18 were excluded as participants due their classification as a "vulnerable group".


Data Analysis

Seven categories of themes and keywords were derived from our research results:

TECHNOLOGY

Software, accessibility, computers, investment

ACADEMICS

Parent-teaching, distractions, IEP, adaptability

MENTAL HEALTH

Social skills, isolation, stress, anxiety

INDIVIDUAL SUCCESS

IEP, engagement, accountability, structure

BEHAVIOURS

Patience, focus, stress, anxiety

ORGANIZATION

Schedule, structure, teacher, Google

CLASS SIZES

Engagement, safety

We noted the following recurring issues related to online learning and the effects of the pandemic on home life:
  • Changes in students' behaviour and mental well-being caused by limited social interaction with peers.
  • Elevated pressure and stress on parents/guardians to help with online learning.
  • Teachers struggling to maintain student engagement and schedule structure during learning times.
  • A decline in overall student success and motivation to learn.

So, how do we solve this?

By integrating an important part of daily school life into video call software — recess.
Our research highlighted a lack of social interaction from peer to peer, caused by social distancing restrictions during the pandemic. Since video call software, such as Zoom, Cisco WebEx, etc., was being predominantly used by school boards and teachers, we chose to design an integrated feature that would allow students to communicate with each other and develop social skills in a way that draws from one of the most vital parts of a regular school day.

This is where Recess Mode comes into play.

Introducing Virtual Recess

Recess Mode is a desktop and tablet feature for Zoom that places the power of social interaction back into the hands of students learning from home. Recess is essential for helping children learn to communicate with others and regulate emotions in social settings; removing this component negatively impacts them in ways that is reflected in their home life (stress, social anxiety, behaviour issues). By incorporating a virtual recess break similar in format to an in-person recess, students are able to recharge and continue learning without experiencing burnout.
Recess is more than just a time to play with friends.
It's an opportunity to grow and see the world from outside of the classroom. Recess Mode helps bridge the gap between social interaction and fun, with activities familiar to students and an emphasis on parallel/group play. From watching movies and drawing, to chatting about the newest cartoons and books, virtual recess becomes a way to bring back the connections made through in-person school days.

Design Requirements

Taking into consideration the limitations of Zoom as an existing software and the range of technological fluency, we derived a set of design requirements for the product:

01

The user must be able to use the interface without requiring help from a parent/guardian.

02

The user should be provided with different “recess” activities to participate in.

03

The user must be able to clearly see all available activities and easily access them when needed.

04

The user should be able to tell when “recess” is over and have constant access to this information.

05

The user must be enticed to participate in activities through age-appropriate themes and designs.

Visual Identity

Recess Mode's visual identity encourages ease of use for children and draws from the playful nature of recess. As a team, we unanimously agreed that the interface would need to reflect the bright colours found in media that is education-related; classrooms also inspired us, as teachers often go above and beyond to foster welcoming environments for their students through decorating their rooms.
The original concept for the feature used an accent colour of turquoise (#99E6E0) in order to remain within the analagous colour range of Zoom's established visual identity. With the re-design, Recess Mode makes use of orange (#F6953B) in the hopes of reflecting the vibrant and playful feeling that recess provides. To differentiate between activities, each activity has its respective colour scheme and mascot. Iconography and buttons are consistent throughout each screen; we selected symbols that could be recognized and understood by younger demographics.
