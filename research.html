Signage Design & Wayfinding Systems: The Toronto Subway’s Fight For Credibility

This thesis examines the history of the Toronto Subway’s signage through a visual study on past signage design in the subway and wayfinding standards for public transportation. It analyzes the ambiguous origins of the subway’s custom typeface, highlights the inconsistent application of commercial typefaces across stations, and reveals the TTC’s attempt to quietly disregard a professionally-designed and tested wayfinding system.
