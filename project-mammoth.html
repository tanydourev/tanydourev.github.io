PROJECT MAMMOTH is an interactive hologram installation about endangered marine species around the world. It integrates hologram technology with audio/visual and motion components, aiming to provide a unique educational experience that all age demographics can enjoy. Additionally, it encourages educators to approach new ways of presenting information about critical topics in a world where digital methods have become highly effective.

As the appointed project lead, I was responsible for managing a team of twelve designers, ensuring the group worked according to the production schedule generated during the planning phase, and acting as a bridge between faculty and students for communications. I was heavily involved in modelling the marine animals we showcased, as well as making environments and setting up camera animations for each render.

Project Mammoth is the first project of its kind to be created in Brock University's Interactive Arts & Sciences program.

"Our students are building an interactive digital media object, which includes digital displays, embedded computing, open-source software and hardware, as well as additive manufacturing. A hologram projector sounds like science fiction, but our students are able to make all this a reality."
- Dr. Aaron Mauro (Brock University)
Source: Brock News

The contents of the installation were modelled in Blender using low-poly techniques. We selected eight marine animals to showcase: the African penguin, the striped smooth hound shark, the blue-footed booby, the Espanola marine iguana, the North Atlantic right whale, the acropora jacquelinae coral, the Hawksbill sea turtle, and the polar bear.

My role as a modeller involved creating four of the eight animal models, in addition to several environments to showcase their habitats. In order to properly apply the physics of holograms to our installation, each animation was then prepared in a video format that would allow for additional adjustments according to the plexiglass prism being used.

A big thank-you goes out to all members of Project Mammoth and those who were involved in making this installation come together: Samantha Savage, Kathleen MacDonald, Will Paskulin, Shannon Kelly, Patrick Arenas, Matthew MacNeil, Rigel Quindipan, Evan Mason, Rogo Jean, Dongjian Wu (Joey) and Xu Yankeer (Coco). Thank you to Dr. Aaron Mauro for his support and guidance throughout the project.
