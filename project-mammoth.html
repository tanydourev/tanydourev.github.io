OVERVIEW /

Project Mammoth installation

Project Mammoth is an interactive hologram installation about endangered marine species around the world. It integrates hologram technology with audio/visual and motion components, aiming to provide a unique educational experience for all age demographics and encouraging educators to approach digital methods of presenting information.

This installation is the first project of its kind to be created in Brock University's Interactive Arts & Sciences program.

CONTRIBUTIONS /

As the appointed project lead, I was responsible for managing a team of twelve designers throughout an eight-month period. I ensured the group worked according to the production schedule generated during the planning phase, was involved in creating process documentation for all assets in the installation, and acted as a bridge between faculty and students for communications.

My role as a modeller involved creating four of the eight animal models: the African penguin, the striped smooth hound shark, the acropora jacquelinae coral, and the Arctic polar bear. I designed several environments to showcase their habitats, in addition to video-based renders that would be displayed on the hologram's plexiglass prism.
